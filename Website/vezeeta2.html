Key Findings & Recommendations

What This Data Tells Us

Five major findings from the analysis — and what patients, doctors, and platforms can do about them.

🔍
Half of Doctors Are Unreviewed
Nearly half of all doctors on the platform have never received a patient review, creating a massive information gap for patients and an enormous cold-start problem for the platform.
43.4%
have zero patient reviews
📍
Location Drives Pricing
A cardiology consultation costs 750 EGP in New Cairo and 400 EGP in Faisal. Area-of-practice is the strongest pricing predictor on the platform — stronger than specialty itself.
750 vs 400
EGP — same specialty, different area
🧠
High Fee ≠ More Reviews
Pearson correlation between fee and review count is 0.028 — effectively zero. Expensive doctors do not attract more patient feedback. Trust and reputation are built outside the price signal.
r = 0.028
fee ↔ reviews correlation
⚕️
Extreme Specialty Scarcity
The market is not balanced. Reproductive Medicine alone has 1,709 doctors while Geriatrics has just 13 and Radiology 36. The top specialty has over 1,700× more practitioners than the bottom — a critical supply gap for an aging population.
1,709×
gap between top and bottom specialty
🗺️
Cairo Dominates Supply
The top 5 areas — New Cairo, Heliopolis, Nasr City, Dokki & Mohandessin, and 6th of October — are all in Greater Cairo and together hold 32.8% of every doctor on the platform. The rest of Egypt is severely underserved.

of all doctors in just 5 Cairo districts
Recommendations

For Patients, Doctors & the Platform

REC 01
Fix the Scarcity Specialties
Geriatrics (13), Radiology (36), and Family Medicine (51) are the most underserved fields for an aging population. These are onboarding priorities — not marketing priorities.
REC 02
Build Area-Weighted Pricing
Any "recommended price" feature should weight area before specialty. The same consultation varies by 2× across neighborhoods — specialty-only benchmarks are misleading.
REC 03
Target Zero-Review Doctors
7,424 doctors have never received a single review. A first-review incentive program — not more general marketing — is where the platform-wide ROI lives.
REC 04
Use Dentistry as an Acquisition Funnel
Second-most-crowded specialty, lowest median fee (200 EGP), shortest wait (14 min). Perfect first-visit hook — margin comes from cross-sell to other specialties.
REC 05
Separate Labs From Specialties
Only 1 laboratory is listed across all 17,119 records. That's not a data gap — it's a whole product line missing. Labs & Scans belong in their own module.
REC 06
Book Early for Cardiology
Cardiology has the longest median wait (26 min), Dentistry the shortest (14 min). For patients, high-demand specialties need advance booking; for doctors, wait time is a churn risk.
About the Project

The Research Behind This

This report is based on a dataset scraped and cleaned from Vezeeta.com — Egypt's largest online healthcare marketplace. The data covers 17,119 doctor listings across dozens of medical specialties and 140 distinct geographic areas from Cairo to Aswan.

The analysis was conducted in Python across four phases: Specialty Analysis, Geographic Distribution, Price Segmentation, and Doctor Performance. All insights are derived from publicly available listing data.
